Conclusions

1. Time Window Criticality

The 0-10s window carries the strongest signal for detecting the effect of interest. The rapid decay observed in subsequent windows indicates that measurement focus should prioritize early temporal dynamics.

2. Assumption Impact

Simulation assumptions have substantial downstream effects on outcomes. The approximately 2x difference between Conservative and Optimistic scenarios demonstrates the importance of sensitivity analyses and robustness checks across different assumption sets.

3. Reliability vs. Sensitivity

More stringent (Conservative) criteria provide higher hypothesis support rates but at the potential cost of sensitivity. Conversely, lenient (Optimistic) criteria show lower support but might detect subtle effects. The choice depends on the research context and priorities.

4. Methodological Recommendations

  • Conduct sensitivity analyses across effect size assumptions
  • Prioritize measurement in the 0-10s critical window
  • Consider composite hypotheses weighting early vs. late effects differently
  • Report results stratified by scenario to demonstrate robustness
